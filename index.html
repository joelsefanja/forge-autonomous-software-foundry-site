02 / Why

Most products fail before the first line of code.

FORGE starts with demand, not a feature list. The platform keeps market evidence, scope, quality checks and owner documentation connected from the first decision.

Problem

Ideas are cheap. Transferable products are not.

A demo can look good and still be impossible to sell, support, audit or hand over. FORGE treats product evidence, quality proof and owner documentation as part of the build.

Method

Build only after the market signal is strong enough.

01Collect repeated pain from public and commercial sources.
02Reduce the scope to one first product promise.
03Ship the smallest version that can be tested, used and explained.
04Document decisions so the product can be operated or transferred.

05 / Fit

For founders who want evidence before speed.

FORGE is useful when the product must become understandable, operable and transferable, not just impressive in a demo.

Best fit
01Solo founders testing narrow software product ideas.
02Agencies packaging repeatable internal tools or niche products.
03Operators who need software that another owner can understand.
Not a fit

Not for vague platform dreams.

FORGE works best when the first product can be narrow, validated and shipped with clear ownership. Broad platforms come later, if the evidence earns them.

Start with one customer problem. Leave with a product path you can build, test and hand over.

Use FORGE to move from market signal to working software without losing the evidence, decisions and operating context behind the product.
