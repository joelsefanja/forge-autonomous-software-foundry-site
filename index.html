01 / Why

Most products fail before the first line of code.

FORGE starts with demand, not a feature list. The goal is to create software that is understandable, testable and ready for someone else to run.

Problem

Ideas are cheap. Transferable products are not.

A demo can look good and still be impossible to sell, support, audit or hand over. FORGE treats product evidence and owner documentation as part of the build.

Method

Build only after the market signal is strong enough.

01Collect repeated pain from public and commercial sources.
02Reduce the scope to one first product promise.
03Ship the smallest version that can be tested, used and explained.
04Document decisions so the product can be operated or transferred.
02 / Process

A clear path from signal to software.

Each step has a concrete output. That keeps the work readable for humans and useful for automation.

01
Discover
Find repeated customer pain in reviews, forums, job posts, issue trackers and niche communities.
02
Validate
Check who pays, what they use now, what is missing and whether the first version can be narrow.
03
Design
Turn the problem into a product brief, user journeys, acceptance criteria and technical decisions.
04
Build
Create the first useful version with tests, accessibility checks, release notes and deployment setup.
05
Launch
Prepare the landing page, pricing, onboarding, payment path and support model.
06
Hand over
Package docs, risks, operating notes, credentials checklist and transfer steps.

04 / Fit

For founders who want evidence before speed.

FORGE is useful when the product must become understandable, operable and transferable, not just impressive in a demo.

Best fit
01Solo founders testing narrow software product ideas.
02Agencies packaging repeatable internal tools or niche products.
03Operators who need software that another owner can understand.
Not a fit

Not for vague platform dreams.

FORGE works best when the first product can be narrow, validated and shipped with clear ownership. Broad platforms come later, if the evidence earns them.
